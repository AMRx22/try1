Procedure steps


Assessment


1. Determine need to assess client's BP.

a. Note risk factors. age, Medi stress.

b. Observe for signs and symptoms of BP alterations headach, Nausca.

c. Determine client's age.

.2 *Assess for factors that normally influence BP: (Age, Sex, Medications, Daily (diurnal) variation, Exercise, Smoking, Stress, Race, Medical condition, Obesity & Temperature)

.3 Determine best site for BP assessment.

4. Determine previous baseline BP (if available)

5. Encourage client to avoid exercise and smoking for 30 minutes before assessment.

6. Arranged and checked the necessary equipment.

7. Positioned client.

8. Explained procedure to client and have client rested for 5 minutes before measurement. a. Palpation Method

9 *Washed Hands

10. Exposed upper arm fully by removing constricting clothing

11. Palpated brachial artery. Positioned cuff 2.5 cm (1 inch) above site of brachial pulsation (antecubital space). • Centered bladder of cuff above artery. Wrapped cuff evenly and snugly around upper arm.

12. Checked the manometer is on vertical position at eve level.

13. *Palpated brachial or radial artery with fingertips of one hand while inflating cuff rapidly to pressure 30mmg above point at which pulse disappears

14. Deflated cuff rapidly and completely

15. Waited one to two minutes before inflating the cuff for the auscultatory method b. Auscultation Method

16. Repeated the steps 1-5 of palpation method.

17. Deflated cuff fully and wait 30 seconds.

18. Placed stethoscope earpieces in ears and be sure sounds are clear, not muffled. (Checked whether stethoscope is functioning)

19. Relocated brachial artery and place bell or diaphragm chest piece over it.

20. Closed valve of pressure bulb

21. Inflated cuff to 30mm Hg above palpated × 30 systolic pressure

22. Slowly released valve and allow mercury to fall at rate of 2 to 3mm Hg/sec.

23. *Noted point on manometer when first clear sound is heard

24. *Continued to deflate cuff gradually, noting point on manometer at which sound disappears in adults.

25. Deflated cuff rapidly and completely. Removed from client's arm.

26. Repeated procedure on other arm after 2 minutes. (If indicated)

27. Assisted client in returning to comfortable position

28. Wiped the cuff with an approved disinfectant

29. Informed client of BP reading (If appropriate).

30. Washed hands


EVALUATION


31. Waited 1 to 2 minutes and repeat assessment. (if inaudible)

32. Tried alternative methods if still inaudible.

33. Compared BP reading with previous baseline. RECORDING AND REPORTING

34. Recorded BP in medical record or flow sheet.

35. Reported abnormal findings to nurse-in- charge or physician.
